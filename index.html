Climate change projections by constituency over Great Britain

Use the drop down menuu to select a weather variable and time period, then hover over the map to find the values for each individual constituency.

These projections are taken from the Met Office's UKCP18 dataset. They represent the changes in climate of the 2030s and 2060s, compared to 1981-2010 (you can also see the values during this baseline period). This data is the average of an 'ensemble' of climate models with different physics that are then downscaled to an spatial grid of 12km. The projections use a scenarios of global carbon emissions called "RCP8.5", which thankfully we are currently not following. The variables that are currently available to look at are:
  • annual temperature
  • winter rainfall (technically this is precipitation, so it also factors in snowfall)
  • summer rainfall
  • number of days experiencing drought per year
  • change in the number of days categorised as dangerous hot
  • maximum temperature on the warmest day of the year
  • rain falling over the wettest 5 days of the year - related to river flooding
  • the proportion of annual rainfall that falls as extremely heavy rates - related to flash flooding
